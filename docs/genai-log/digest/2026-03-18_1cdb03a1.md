## Summary

This session opened with the spec-tightening work begun at the end of the prior session and ran for twelve days, encompassing the largest concentration of design and implementation work in the project's early history. Lane directed a deep parallel research campaign covering session types theory, Plan 9 systems, BeOS/Haiku internals, NeXTSTEP/early Aqua, wio, extensibility ecosystems, Wayland, Linux subsystems, Nix as a distro base, vintage Unix multi-user UX, and the Be Newsletter archive. From this foundation, Lane reviewed and extensively revised a foundations document and an architecture spec — eliminating a central pane-route server in favor of kit-level routing, adding a scripting protocol grounded in session types and optics, and establishing s6-nix as the concrete base. A Be-systems-engineer agent provided deep review of both specs against Haiku source. The session then shifted to implementation: Stages 0–6 of pane-app (protocol types, Tag builder, Handler/Filter/Looper, App/MockCompositor/hello-pane, routing stubs, BeAPI modernization, session-typed handshake), a working unix-socket transport with calloop integration, a pane-comp Phase 4 Wayland compositor skeleton, and a comprehensive BeAPI naming/idiom audit spanning multiple commits.

## Key directives

- The spec/analysis from the initial research was biased by what pane already was rather than the philosophical ideas that are the most potent; specific design choices at that granularity are misleading at this stage — focus on principles, not feature mappings.
- The research reports mistakenly mapped concepts one-to-one ("BMessage maps to PaneMessage"); this was corrected: the spirit of the inquiry is to understand philosophical ideas and design convictions, not to produce a feature-mapping table.
- Re-run all three research agents (Plan 9, BeOS/Haiku, session types) with web access enabled and with clearer framing; also add NeXTSTEP/early Aqua, the wio project, extensibility ecosystems (Emacs, plugins), and the Be Newsletter archive.
- BeOS is the north star; many other cool ideas from contemporary designs left in the past may be tastefully integrated — their time has arrived in 2026.
- The Cell grid spec has confused us too many times; reformulate or remove it in line with the guiding principles the Be engineers clarified.
- Panes should be thought of as objects in a category whose morphisms are interactions; the "everything is a pane" uniformity principle may yield interesting results — be bold about breaking from conventional design patterns.
- Do not foreground the category theory; keep it to a brief aside for those in the know; panes have many ways to view them (display to a user, fs metadata interface, sockets etc.) — research optics for thinking about how this works coherently.
- Consider whether calloop is optimal; the single-threaded nature worries me compared to BeOS's multi-threaded inspiration — are there alternatives, or how close to BeOS's model can we be over Linux?
- Agents should be modeled as additional users in the system, not as a pane type; take direct inspiration from Plan 9's distributed computing model; manage agents in sandboxed user environments with permissions via declarative specification; intra-system email (the old "You have mail" Unix pattern) as an actual agent communication protocol.
- BeOS is my north star; it is the right timing for asserting this vision; pane is named metaphorically — transparent, its purpose is to let you clearly see what it presents.
- I think we should rewrite all other specs wholesale based on the vision expressed in foundations and architecture; for each spec, review the Be Newsletter for guidance, tempered by Linux's specific capacities.
- The router in the architecture is a single point of failure that contradicts the rest of the document; it must be eliminated; routing is a kit-level concern, not a server.
- Clear out all stale specifications that need more than an estimated 30% rewrite.
- For the Be-engineer architecture draft, give them full creative latitude grounded in foundations + s6-nix + Wayland compositor + Rust; ask how Be engineers would proceed.
- Questions on the two-world problem: our hope is BeAPI's design model will yield good dev UX, but the strategy accounts for gradual adoption; the ext4 xattr limit means we simply won't support ext4 as an installation target (opinionated base stack gives us leeway).
- Implement pane-app Stages 0–6 in sequence: protocol types, Tag builder, Handler/Filter/Looper, App/MockCompositor end-to-end, routing stubs, BeAPI modernization, session-typed handshake.
- Phase 4 compositor: build pane-comp as a real Wayland compositor using smithay; use a linux-builder NixOS VM for testing; integrate calloop.
- Do a full BeAPI naming audit; then a Rust idiom audit; then a stale documentation audit; fix all findings across the codebase.
- Prepare handoff: current state in serena, MEMORY.md points to serena as authoritative; add PLAN.md as a living roadmap with session start/end checklists.
