# Don't accept design weaknesses as-is without Lane's review

**Rule:** Roundtable verdicts of "accept as-is" for design properties must be presented to Lane for review, not treated as final.

Don't let roundtable "accept as-is" verdicts become final without Lane's review. In session 2, the roundtable unanimously accepted S9 (session non-recycling, 254 limit) and S8 (postcard type confusion) as "fine." Lane pushed back: "I am distrustful of both of the framings that these are things we can either not care about or put off for later. We want correct by construction/design guarantees wherever possible." Both items got fixed.

**Why:** Lane's design standard is correct-by-construction, not correct-by-convention. Agent roundtables can be too comfortable with "the routing is correct so this is fine" or "254 is enough." These are convention-based arguments that Lane rejects.

**How to apply:** When presenting roundtable verdicts, flag "accept as-is" items as needing Lane's explicit approval. Don't frame them as resolved — frame them as "the roundtable recommends accepting, here's why, here's the counter-argument."
